My Investing Philosophy

Focus on Value

Money is made in investing by figuring out what something is worth and paying a lot less. I only invest in businesses that are undervalued. I screen and analyze thousands of companies, but I only buy when the price is low enough that it may provide a satisfactory return and at the same time offer a wide margin of safety.

I place an incredible amount of time and analysis on determining the range of potential outcomes of an investment. I not only look for high returns, but I also look at the risk. I analyze every knowable fact about the business and I talk to management and to all the parties involved in transactions with the company. That means that I also look for potential hidden assets and off-balance-sheet liabilities that enable me to arrive at risk-adjusted valuations. I build multiple scenarios based on financial, operational and legal analysis, and invest when I believe that there is enough downside protection and considerable upside.

My competitive advantages include:
• Long-term orientation – Allowing focus on absolute returns and hence maximum profit.
• Education in Finance and Law – Allowing participation in the distressed market and international financial instruments, and enhancing protection against downside.
• Capability to speak multiple languages – Allowing access to international markets and more in-depth due diligence and understanding of important subtleties.
• Focus on Value – Avoiding investing in overvalued businesses and fads.
• Involvement in Technology – Allowing better understanding of new technologies and how they affect the economy, keeping updated on new developments and capability of valuing technology businesses.
• Experience in Real Estate – Allowing optimum investment and management of properties globally. These competitive advantages have allowed me to successfully invest internationally, in different parts of the capital structure and to make money in all market conditions.


Ultimate Goal

My goal is to buy whole businesses, whether through the acquisition of large amounts of stock, through the acquisition of debt, or through purchases in the private sector. In terms of real estate, my goal is to continue to buy and own multi-unit properties. While my beginnings were in the public markets, as my assets under management continue to grow, I will expand into the private sector. My investments then will be in both the public and private markets.
